Effects of Income Ranges and Company Ratings on loans at Prosper¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to focus on the relationships Income Range and the Prosper Rating had with other factors in the dataset, especially the borrower rate

Dataset Overview¶

The Data consisted of data of 113,937 loans ranging from 2005-2014 with 81 different variables for each loan. This investigation focused on only 14 of those variables. A high Prosper Rating means an individual is safer or less likely to default on a loan

Distribution of Income Range¶

Most of the individuals in the Dataset were located between the income ranges of 25,000 Dollars to 74,999 Dollars. There were a minimal number of people stated as unemployed or earning no income. There were also 7600 people who did not have their income range displayed

Prosper Rating vs Borrower Rate¶

There is a negative correlation between the Prosper Rating and the Borrower Rate, the higher or less risky the Prosper rating becomes, the lower the average Borrower Rate becomes. This is a very common act for loan companies

Borrower Rate by Year and Income Range¶

The Average Borrower Rate becomes lower as the income range becomes higher, this also points to the fact that a borrowers Prosper Rating is likely to be higher as their income range increases. There seems to have been a spike in the borrower rates between 2011 and 2012 but the rates have been falling since then in most cases apart from unemplyed or zero income individuals who have experienced a higher spike in their borrower rates.

Borrower Rate by Income Range and Home Owner Status¶

The average borrower rate is significantly lower for borrowers who own homes. This could be interpreted as the loan company predicting they are safer options and could also have a high value collateral asset in the case of defaulting on the loan. The borrower rate also follows the same theme as above in reducing as the income range increases but a point of interest is the average rate for borrowers with zero income who seem to have a lower rate than individuals earning between 1 and 49,999 Dollars.
